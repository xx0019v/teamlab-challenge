02 — MANIFESTO
P.02
02
Manifesto

The interface
should disappear
into the experience.

It should guide only when needed, reduce visual noise, and allow the artwork to remain the center of attention.

必要なときだけ導き、視覚的なノイズを減らし、作品が体験の中心であり続けるための背景になる。

01
Low Brightness
For dark exhibition spaces, the UI uses restrained contrast and soft light. A single Water Blue is the only accent.
暗い空間に合わせ低輝度・低彩度で構成。アクセントは Water Blue 一色のみに絞っている。
02
Less Text During Immersion
While visitors are looking at artworks, only direction, distance, and a short hint remain on screen.
作品を観ている間は情報量を絞り、方向・距離・短いヒントだけを残す。
03
Movement-first Guidance
Large tap areas, short directions, and vibration-based cues support walking visitors, one hand at a time.
大きなタップ領域・短い指示・振動の合図で、歩きながらの来場者を支える。

03 — EXPERIENCE FLOW
P.03
Experience Flow

Five quiet moments,
from anticipation
to memory.

The exhibition experience does not start at the entrance. It begins at home and continues after the visit.

展示体験は入口で始まらず、出口で終わらない。自宅で準備をはじめ、訪問後にも余韻として続く。

01 — STAGE
Before Visit
Prepare ticket and basic information.
チケットと基本情報を、当日の前に静かに整える。
02 — STAGE
Entering
Reduce decisions at the entrance.
入口での迷いを減らし、意識を作品に渡す。
03 — STAGE
Exploring
Guide nearby artworks without overwhelming the visitor.
近くの作品を、押しつけがましくなく案内する。
04 — STAGE
Immersing
Keep the screen quiet during the artwork experience.
作品を体験している間、画面は静かなままでいる。
05 — STAGE
Remembering
Let visitors revisit the experience after leaving.
展示を出たあとも、静かに振り返れる余韻をつくる。

04 — DESIGN SYSTEM
P.04
Design System

Tokens,
not values.

Colors, type, spacing, and radii live in Variables and Styles. Screens never hold raw values. The system extends the Figma main file and is named so it travels into code without re-interpretation.

06 — WHAT CHANGED
P.06
What Changed

Eight quiet decisions,
not a redesign.

The structure of the existing app is kept. What changes is what the app shows, when it shows it, and how loudly. Each decision below is small. Together they let the artwork stay in the center.

既存の構造は変えていません。変えたのは「何を、いつ、どのくらいの強さで見せるか」。小さな判断の重なりで、主役を作品に戻すための設計です。

01
Lower brightness for dark spaces.
Deep black surfaces and a single Water Blue accent prevent the screen from competing with the artwork.
深い黒と1色のアクセントだけで、画面が作品と競合しない。
02
Less text during immersion.
In front of an artwork, only direction, distance, and one short hint remain on screen.
作品の前では、方向・距離・短いヒントだけが残る。
03
Larger tap areas while walking.
All interactive targets are 44pt or larger with 8pt spacing — usable in motion, in low light.
タップ領域は44pt以上、間隔8pt以上。歩きながらでも誤タップしにくい。
04
Vibration-based guidance.
Approach, turn and arrival are communicated through short haptic patterns instead of sound or long text.
接近・曲がる・到着を、音や長文ではなく短い振動で伝える。
05
Crowd-aware routing.
Crowd Balance reads live density and suggests a quieter path, distributing visitors across the venue.
混雑度を読み、空いている順路を提案して全体の流れを整える。
06
Reusable UI components.
Topbar, Audio Player, Floating Current, Bottom Sheet, Button, Tag and Pin are shared across all 12 screens.
主要コンポーネントを12画面で共通利用している。
07
Structured design system.
Colors, type, spacing, radius and motion are managed through Variables and Styles, not raw values per screen.
色・タイポ・余白・角丸・モーションを Variable と Style で管理。
08
Visitor-first hierarchy.
Each screen carries one clear intent. The most important action is always within the visitor's thumb reach.
各画面に1つの意図。主要アクションは常に親指の届く範囲に。
